
Chocolate Pizzelles~These are thin waferlike cookies and you must have a pizzelle iron to make them. The Italians call them "Pizzelle Alle Nocciole".~1/4 cup cocoa powder`1 cup white sugar`1/4 teaspoon salt`3/4 cup ground hazelnuts`1 tablespoon baking powder`2 cups all-purpose flour`1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon`1 cup unsalted butter`4 eggs`~Cool on racks. Sprinkle with powdered sugar.`Stir in the hazelnuts last.`Heat Pizzelle iron and place 1 teaspoon of batter on each imprint. Close iron and bake 30 seconds.`Whisk the eggs with the sugar and salt until light. Melt the butter and stir in.`Sift together all the remaining ingredients except the hazelnuts and fold in.`~
Peter Pan Cookies~Great for after school snacks.~1 cup peanut butter`2 eggs`1 cup white sugar`2 cups sifted all-purpose flour`1 tablespoon milk`1 teaspoon baking soda`1 cup shortening`1/2 teaspoon salt`1 cup packed brown sugar`~Combine shortening, salt, and peanut butter and mix well. Add sugars gradually and cream thoroughly. Add beaten eggs and milk. Mix well.`Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (170 degrees C).`Sift flour with soda and add to first mixture, blending well.`Drop from teaspoon on greased baking sheet. Press with fork or fancy glass. Bake 15-20 minutes`~
Pavlova~My grandmother's recipe from New Zealand for the famous Pavlova.~1 teaspoon vanilla extract`1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar`3 teaspoons cornstarch`3 tablespoons cold water`1 cup super fine sugar`3 egg whites`~Place on parchment paper on a greased baking sheet and bake for 45 minutes. Turn off the oven and leave cookies to cool in the oven. NOTE: Don't go opening and closing the oven door. If you must, close the door very carefully. Pavlova is not individual servings, it is of cake-size, decorated with whipped cream and topped with fresh fruit of your choice just before serving.`Preheat oven to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C).`Slow beater and add vinegar, vanilla and cornstarch.`Beat egg whites until stiff, add cold water and beat again. Add sugar very gradually while still beating.`~
Nana Smith's Oatmeal Biscuits~Old-style English oatmeal cookies, more like a bar, very popular in the 1950's, at least in our house.~1 cup self-rising flour`1 cup rolled oats`1/2 cup white sugar`1/2 cup butter`1 ounce toasted sesame seeds (Optional)`~Bake for 20 minutes (until lightly brown). Cool in pan, cutting into slices after a minute or so cooling, then leaving in pan until completely cool.`Melt butter or margarine in saucepan, then mix with other ingredients. Toast sesame seeds in oven or grill, being very careful to avoid burning them. Mix with other ingredients and stir well. Press into greased tin, should be about a half inch thick.`Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C). Grease 9 x 9 inch pan.`~
Evelyn's Cornflake Cookies~I got this recipe from my ex mother-in-law in the late 6O's.~2 cups crushed cornflakes cereal`24 maraschino cherries`1/4 teaspoon salt`1/4 teaspoon almond extract`1 cup all-purpose flour`1/2 cup white sugar`1/2 cup butter`3 ounces cream cheese`1 teaspoon baking powder`~Roll into 1 inch balls, then roll balls into crumbs. Place on parchment-lined sheets, top each cookie with a cherry. Bake for 13 minutes; 6 minutes on middle rack, 7-8 minutes on top rack.`Cream together all ingredients except cornflakes and cherries. Chill dough for one hour.`Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C).`~
Praline Cookies~This recipe makes a good cookie, but rather rich!~1/4 teaspoon salt`2/3 cup packed brown sugar`1/2 teaspoon maple flavored extract`1 teaspoon vanilla extract`1 egg`1/2 teaspoon baking soda`1 cup pecan halves`1/2 cup butter`2 cups all-purpose flour`~Slice dough into 1/4 inch slices. Place slices on cookie sheet. Top each cookie with a pecan half. Bake for 10 minutes in preheated oven, or until golden brown.`In a large bowl, cream together butter and brown sugar. Beat in egg, vanilla and maple flavoring. Beat in salt and flour until a smooth dough is formed. Shape dough into a log 12 inches long, wrap in wax paper, and refrigerate overnight.`Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C). Line cookie sheet with parchment paper.`~
Brown Sugar Frosting~Great on Praline Cookies (see recipe)~1 tablespoon white corn syrup`2 tablespoons water`1/2 cup packed dark brown sugar`1 cup confectioners' sugar`~Cook all ingredients except confectioner's sugar in iron skillet until smooth. Remove and add sugar. Spoon/drizzle over cookies. Frosting will harden when cool!`~
